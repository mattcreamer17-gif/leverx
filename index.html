The thesis

Scoping discipline is the 2026 differentiator.

LeverX is carrying a Joule deck, a treasury case with J.P. Morgan, a Databricks partnership, and twenty years of continuous SAP work going back to 2003. When a client asks for "agentic AI in SAP," the real question is which parts of that ask actually belong in Joule, which belong in BTP workflow orchestration, which belong in rule-based config inside S/4HANA, and which belong in a custom ABAP layer that no AI should touch. A practice lead can answer that in a room. A firm of 500 certified consultants answering that consistently across every engagement is a different problem.

Eduba works on the layer underneath the stack choice. The 60/30/10 frame says that in most enterprise problems, 60% of the work is traditional code and data plumbing, 30% is rule-based logic, and 10% is a genuine AI problem where a model earns its place. Applied to SAP: 60% of what a client calls "AI" is really BTP workflow orchestration or clean ABAP extensions. 30% is rules and config inside S/4HANA. 10% is where Joule and agentic AI belong.

The framework does not replace Joule. It tells a practice lead which piece of a scope goes in Joule and which pieces do not, so that the Joule piece works, the rest of the scope holds, and the engagement closes on margin.

The vehicle is co-delivery or white-label methodology licensing. LeverX consultants keep delivering the SAP work. Eduba brings the framework and the published provenance behind it, so that every practice lead can scope Joule and BTP engagements the same way the next one does.
